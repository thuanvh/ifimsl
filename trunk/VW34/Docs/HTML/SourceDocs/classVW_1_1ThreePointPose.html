Detailed Description

Compute 3D pose of 3 points given their image projections.

Author:
ID Reid
Date:
30/11/04 Determine the depths, locations and pose of three points given their image projections. Uses the 3-point method of Fischler and Bolles (which involves solving a degree-4 polynomial) to obtain possible depths of points. These depths lead directly to point locations, and can be used to determine the pose of the three points relative to an alternative coordinate frane using the method of Faugeras to solve for a quaternion rotation
Author:
ID Reid
Date:
30/11/04 Determine the depths, locations and pose of three points given their image projections. Uses the 3-point method of Fischler and Bolles (which involves solving a degree-4 polynomial) to obtain possible depths of points. These depths lead directly to point locations, and can be used to determine the pose of the three points relative to an alternative coordinate frame using the method of Faugeras to solve for a quaternion rotation

Optional computation of Jacobian. Done numerically via pertubations.
